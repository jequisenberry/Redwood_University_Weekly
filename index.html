Spring Semester Kicks Off ! As dawn filters through the towering sequoias, nearly 15,000 students return to Redwood State University for Spring 2025. The Quad hums with newfound energy as winter's quiet gives way to freshmen orientation, study groups gathering beneath rustling branches, and faculty igniting research projects across campus. Campus Life Director Maya Chen kicked things off with a spring BBQ on the Quad Lawn—complete with local snacks and live acoustic music. “Gathering under these giants reminds us who we are,” Chen said. “This semester, we're all about sustainability, wellness, and shared experiences.” In academics, the School of Environmental Sciences launches a sequoia-resilience study in partnership with local conservation groups, while the Arts Department unveils its first student-curated gallery in the renovated Redwood Hall Annex. With solar-powered charging benches dotting the Quad and new late-night shuttle routes, RSVPs are already open for February's Career Fair and Valentine's Open Mic. Whether you seek internships, quiet study spots, or community events, spring at Redwood State promises something for everyone.
Redwood Grove Renovation
The cherished Redwood Grove study area has been refreshed for spring: reclaimed-timber picnic tables now flank new USB-C charging posts, and gentle strand lighting stretches between the trunks to keep late-night study sessions aglow. Look for “Forest Nook” markers along the quad's east edge and settle in beneath the canopy for your next group project or solo reading hour.
